Who am I

I'm Nick Bender, I'm a computer science student at the University of Colorado Boulder. I build and learn. I love to learn and my favorite way to learn is by building. A lot of my repositories are records of me learning something new or they are for school work. There are some more cheeky projects in there too, mosty because I found myself with some free time and an itch to build something. That combination often doesn't occur with a (at the time of writing this) strong interest in robotics and artificial intelligence.

How I got here

Where I am now started with a joke during summer school when I joked about building a small robot to roam around the engineering building at CU and knock over trash cans. I deigned and built a crude prototype, then a professor me to to take a robotics class. The robotics class had us build software to race a small car around a hall way. In the class we learned about core robotics concepts and we had to build path finding algorithms, kalman filters, and even implement realtime object detection. Autonomy is one hell of a drug. I ended up as part of the CU sub terranian challenge team and taking another robotics class where we were allowed to design and build our own robot and our own midterm and final. Taking the knowledge from the previous class we built a robot that would move around our campus autonomously. Through reading for this class I also found myself deeply interested in deep reinforcement learning and its application to robotics. Thats where My story ends in the fall of 2019, however there is adventure on the horizon, so I know this section will get longer in the future.

Lucky #7

This was a project for advanced robotics where we automated a small remote control car using cameras, lidar, and a small computer to drive around a section of hall way as part of a class competition. We used ROS for coordinating control of the car and all of the car's components. The project taught us about IOT computers, ROS, SLAM, kalman filters, power management, and camera systems to name some of our bigger struggles and some of the larger lessons we learned.

Capable Octopus

This project was a project as part of the autonomous vehicle competition class where we took the option of devising our own project to work on instead of making a small autonomous racecar. The robot platform we used was a clearpath jackal, for cumpute resources we used an intel nuc and an nvidia agx xavier. For sensors we used 3 of intel's d430 realsense cameras and gps. The goal of the project was to make the robot autonomousely navigate around our campus. For control we used a semantic segmentation network to follow sidewalks and stereo slam to map our campus. This project also helped some of the team members work on their capstone projetcs. For me it taught me more about segmentation networks, reinforcement learning, and it hardened my knowledge of ROS.

MARBLE GUI

This is not a project I started but I intend to finish. I am working on the gui for managing CU's robotic deployment at the DARPA sub terranian challenge. I have been optimizing what exists and expanding functonality. The challenge that the GUI has to overcome is that it has to help the mission comander manage many robots with relative ease. The robots report artifcats and artifact locations during the event and the GUI fuses those artifact locations then sends that information to DARPA's server. Seeing what each robot has reported can become overwhelming when there are 10 robots, I try to make that amout of information managable.
